In Miscellaneous Order

If you have a Computer

  • Protect Your Eyes, You Can’t Replace Them
    • Check out an app like f.lux to adjust the temperature of your display with day-night, reducing eye strain and affect on sleep cycles.
    • Check out an app like “Awareness” or “Time Out” (Mac only) that reminds you to take a break every 10-20 minutes to exercise your eyes and body. (There’s the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes take 20 seconds to look at something 20 feet away.)
  • Mouse If your mouse has a lot of friction, check out PTFE tape to put on the underside.
  • Backing Up What would you loose if the drive on your computer (or phone) failed (or was lost)? Aim to follow the 3-2-1 backup rule: 3 copies of your data on 2 media types with 1 copy offsite.

If you have a Mac

  • In Finder:
    • Use the combination command and the up arrow key (⌘↑) to go up (left) a column (especially if you are in a Save As… dialog and it won’t let you go further to the left without going to the dropdown menu).
    • Double click on column dividers to right-size them or right click on them for more options.
  • Try out a window manager, I recommend Spectacle, you can easily put two windows side-by-side etc with some keyboard shortcuts.

Visualizing 4D Regular Polytopes: Hypernom

There are five convex regular polytopes in three dimensions: the Platonic Solids: tetrahedron, cube, octahedron, dodecahedron, and icosahedron. In four dimensions, there are six. All higher dimensions only have three. (In two dimensions, there are infinitely many: all the regular polygons). A full list of regular polytopes (including those that are not convex and those that are not finite) is here

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_regular_polytopes_and_compounds

http://hypernom.com/ is a wonderful visualization/game (created by Vi Hart et al) taking place on the (three dimensional) boundaries of the six convex regular polytopes in four dimensions.
